Combat System Builder

CSB is a tool for developing auto-battling turn-based combat systems for RPG games.

To start, head to the Ruleset section to customize your system's mechanics - there you will be able to tweak variables responsible for the behaviour of attributes used by the characters in the Stat Sheet section.

In the Stat Sheet section you can prepare two fighters with attributes that are described by the ruleset.

CSB offers possibility to create custom abilities that will be used during simulation. They are described by their name, physical or magical attack type, damage spread and chance of occurrence in contrast of other abilities of the same type.

Additionally, CSB supports a tool for balancing the fighters called combat value. In the Ruleset section, each attribute can have assigned a combat value. Combat value present in the Stat Sheet section is the sum of those values multiplied by their respective attributes, giving user an estimate about the capabilities of the fighter. In a well-balanced combat system, fighters of similar combat values should have equal chances at winning against each other unless a counter strategy is applied.

The author of this website is Bartosz Derenda.

This website is an integral part of the Bachelor thesis, major in Electronic Information Processing, conducted under the supervision of dr hab. Janusz Jurek, prof. UJ at the Faculty of Management and Social Communication of the Jagiellonian University.

The aim of this thesis is to build an application that could aid game developers in creating and balancing turn-based combat systems, which could be used both in tabletop and virtual games. It tackles the problem of balancing a game outside the usual scope of tweaking the game variables that the player can interact with during gameplay.
